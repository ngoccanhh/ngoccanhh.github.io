Does surveillance camera technology in China create up to 4 billionaires of dollars?



Auhor: Tramht
Privacy: Public

Someone from cycling to work, now has a luxury car and even donates BMW to junior staff. Before becoming a billionaire, Dai Lin frequently rode his car to work at Tiandy Technologies Co's headquarters, a company specializing in manufacturing surveillance cameras for the Chinese government. When he opened his company in 1994, surveillance cameras were still something strange to the people here, but so far they are everywhere. China's strict people's management policies are the main reason for people like Mr. Dai Lin to become billionaires.


Now Mr. Lin is 54 years old, takes a car, and is willing to spend his money to buy high-end BMWs for junior staff. His company is now thriving, with the largest customer being the Chinese government.According to Bloomberg, Tiandy has a value of up to 1.4 billion USD.

These huge numbers show the government's policies President Xi Jinping to monitor 1.4 billion people here. In 2016, there were 176 million surveillance cameras installed on streets in China, to compare,this figure in the US is only 50 million. In 2017, the government spent $ 184 million on defense. By 2020, the government plans to develop a 24/7 tracking system, along with a system to assess people's scores, which can be rewarded depending on each person's behavior.
