Crushing iPhone into powder, scientists analyze each ingredient in it

Recently, a team of Plymouth University scientists used a blender to crush iPhone 4S to find a list of ingredients inside an iPhone. They discovered many unexpected things!

You probably don't take the time to think about what kind of material your phone is made of. You should probably know, because our phones look so simple, neat, but they are actually an incredibly complex and interesting combination with many kinds of metals and minerals throughout the Earth.

Recently, a team of Plymouth University scientists tried to find a list of ingredients to create a smartphone, to raise awareness about the impact of these devices on the environment and humanity. . To begin with, they did a rather "violent" act: using a blender to crush the iPhone 4S and analyze the elements inside it.

The process of "decoding" elements inside an iPhone begins with crushing it. After being ground into fine dust and small pieces, the materials are mixed with sodium peroxide and put into a furnace at a temperature of 480 ° C. Dijkstra explains that sodium peroxide will oxidize metals, so they can be dissolved with dilute nitric acid. Then, they will use the emission spectrometer to analyze the metal composition in that solution.
